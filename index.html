About Me

My name is Danielle and I'm a first year student, studying Interactive Media Design at Algonquin College. This is not my first time studying at a post secondary institution. I attended UofO and UW to study Sociology. However, in 2005, I decided to pause my academic pursuits in favour of motherhood. I made this decision three courses shy of graduating with a Bachelors of Sociology, minor in Womenâ€™s Studies. What I missed out on in university, I made up for with a career that span nearly twenty years, all spent doing work the INSPIRED ME working with an amazing community with advocates from various a not for profits and charities.

I've worked in schools, shelters, youth drop in centres, health care settings and canvassed door to door. Regardless of the setting, each work assignment promised a litinany of lessons learned from an eclectic mix of characters. Consequently, I was privy to many adventures and more often than not training felt like baptism by fire. Not that I minded, with a sensory processing palette that favours BIG, BRIGHT and BOLD flavours with a tendency for risk taking. This coupled with attention and memory deficits, I preferred the variety of contract work boredom and on the edge of your seat/risk taking and the opposite of risk adverse.I learned to rig lighting with a gittery roady, touring with Our Lady Peace, photographed nervous newbie nude models, did my best to appear confident and remain calm, while working door and collecting drugs, sharps, and twice a stolen hand gun, working the door at an inner city drop inwith artists and researchers. Although, the job title I most identify with, is Story Teller or sometimes Story Keeper. Over the the years I've written stacks of grants and proposals. Writing a good proposal, means telling a compelling story and concurrently mapping out the mandate and history of an organization or project. It is one of my favourite aspects of working in this sector. That and working for organizations committed to elevating the voices of those most impacted by adversity. Whether it be grassroots community organizing or policy development and reform - the sharing of one's lived experience is paramount to action and necessary to inform meaningful change.
